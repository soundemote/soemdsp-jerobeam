# Distribution research (2026-07-04)

Goal stated by the rights holder: maximize attention and usage over
profit for this release. Full research ran as a multi-agent web-search
+ adversarial-verification pass; findings below are the claims that
survived verification (2/3+ vote), with confidence noted.

## Where to post (verified)

1. **Jerobeam Fenderson's own audience** -- the "oscilloscope music"
   community he built around oscilloscopemusic.com, Patreon, YouTube,
   and Bandcamp. He has direct precedent for selling/releasing novel,
   code-driven executable creative tools to this exact audience (a
   500-copy limited floppy-disk release bundling executable
   audio/visual generator programs). Highest-confidence, highest-fit
   first announcement target.

2. **The Max/MSP Discord** (community-run by James Bradbury, not
   Cycling '74 itself, active since 2017, for sharing patches). Since
   these are direct Gen~/Max patch ports, this is a technically
   literate audience that already knows the originals.

## Unverified but plausible (research hit rate-limit errors verifying
these -- not disproven, just unconfirmed; check manually before relying
on them)

- `awesome-webaudio` / `awesome-web-audio` GitHub lists
- CreativeApplications.Net (creative-coding editorial site)
- "Web Audio Weekly" newsletter
- An annual Web Audio Conference

## Pricing

Confirmed: pay-what-you-want should be treated as a reach/discovery
mechanism, not a revenue plan (itch.io community consensus: "you should
not expect money [from PWYW], that is not the right mindset"). This is
consistent with the stated goal but does NOT prove PWYW drives more
downloads than a fixed price -- that specific causal claim did not
survive verification. Recommendation given the stated goal: switch to
PWYW with $0 minimum, since it removes the one real friction point and
loses nothing measurable against the goal.
